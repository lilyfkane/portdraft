Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Creating Mindful Habits

I am so excited to take this class because I haven’t been pushed to reach my creative potential in a long time. I am a stranger to coding and most digital content creation, so I am eager to learn these valuable skills. So far, getting into the habit of going outside and practicing my photography and videography skills has been very rewarding and eye opening. Although these assignments count for a grade, they mean much more to me. They encourage me to go outside of my comfort zone, and learn useful skills outside my major. As an environmental studies major, I have limited experience in computer skills, so learning HTML coding is very exciting.

2. Collaborating With Peers

Having the opportunity to work with so many talented students has been a treat. It is nice to collaborate as a team and offer insight based on our different areas of expertise. One of the greatest parts of this class is that everyone teaches everyone something new! We all know how to utilize different tools and applications, so we are all one giant resource for one another. As someone with little experience in digital composition, I feel lucky to learn from my peers. I am constantly impressed with the content they create for this class and find it to be a massive source of inspiration in my academic and creative life. Many of my classmates are D&ID or computer science majors, students I don’t usually get to work with.

3. The Art of Craft

I recently purchased a professional camera because I’ve wanted to get into photography for a while now. Signing up for this class was the final push I needed to make the investment because I knew I would have many opportunities to practice and learn about the art. I enjoy exploring websites like Coolors, which offers hundreds of color pallets for HTML. W3 is another great resource that offers tutorials for HTML. As the semester goes on, I find myself looking for every opportunity to snap a photo or document a beautiful moment. I’m enjoying looking for new angles and different perspectives around campus, while learning the importance of patience.

4. Research and Skill Development

The biggest challenge for me so far has been learning coding in HTML. However, it has been really rewarding learning these new skills and realizing that computer science is for everyone! I've enjoyed messing around with different colors, sizing, and fonts within my HTML websites. I have found that utilizing online sources like YouTube and TikTok has been a great way to navigate the applications we are learning in class, such as Bootstrap and Adobe. There is an endless amount of knowledge and tutorials out there, and though it may seem complicated at first, it is all part of the learning process. As a kid, I loved messing around with creative platforms like iMovie, but I lost touch with that as I got older and school became more demanding. It has been so fun revisiting these old passions of mind and applying them to the real world, as well as things I could use in my future career.

5. Presenting my Creations

I am relatively comfortable with public speaking but haven’t had to do it in a while, so I’m a bit rusty. I am more nervous about sharing my creative work with others, something I haven’t done since I was young. Artwork is a scary thing to share because it really is so subjective. The students in this classroom are kind, helpful, and overall it is a no-judgment space, so I know I will be able to do it.
